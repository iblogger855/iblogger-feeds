##១. រឿងប្រស្នា (The Parable)

### English

Once upon a time, there was a great hospital in Lyon that cared for thousands of patients. The hospital director, Dr. Bernard, was a visionary — he wanted to move all patient records to the cloud, to make them accessible to every doctor, from every ward, at any time of day.

He called a meeting with his CTO, his CISO, and three cloud vendors. He asked each vendor one question: *"Are you secure?"*

All three said yes.

He chose the cheapest one.

The vendor showed him certificates: ISO 27001, SOC 2 Type II, PCI-DSS. "We have passed every audit," the salesperson said. "Our data centres are hardened. Our network is encrypted. We are trusted by Fortune 500 companies."

Dr. Bernard was satisfied. He signed.

For two years, everything worked perfectly.

Then one afternoon, an inspector from ANS arrived at the hospital with a clipboard. She walked through the IT department, asking questions. She left three hours later and mailed a formal notice: **The vendor was not HDS certified. The hospital had been illegally hosting patient data for two years.**

Dr. Bernard called the vendor. "But you said you were secure!"

"We are secure," the salesperson replied. "We just are not HDS certified. That is a different thing."

Dr. Bernard did not understand the difference — and that was the problem.

**Security** means the castle walls are thick and the guards are trained.  
**HDS** means the French government has inspected the castle, confirmed its location is on French soil, tested that the drawbridge can be lowered to let the king's data out if the king ever wants to leave, and issued a royal licence saying this castle is worthy of holding the kingdom's most precious secrets.

A castle can be secure without having the royal licence. But in France, the royal licence is the law. You cannot hold patient data without it, no matter how thick your walls are.

Dr. Bernard had to migrate the entire system. It cost three times the money he had saved by choosing the cheaper vendor. Two senior IT staff resigned. The hospital's insurance premiums doubled.

And the patients — who trusted the hospital with the most private information they possessed — never knew. They never knew that for two years, their deepest medical truths had been sitting in a data centre that had no legal right to hold them.

## ២. មេរៀន (The Lesson)

### English

**Security is not compliance. Compliance is not security. In France, for health data, you need both — and they are verified separately.**

The parable reveals three traps every engineer must avoid:

**Trap 1: Certificate Confusion**  
ISO 27001 certifies *how you manage information security*. SOC 2 certifies *controls for service organisations*. HDS certifies *that you are legally authorised to host French health data*. These are different certificates for different purposes. Having one does not grant another.

**Trap 2: Delegation Fallacy**  
"My cloud provider is certified, therefore I am certified." This is the most common and most expensive mistake. Cloud certifications cover the infrastructure layer. Application-layer certification — which covers the code you deploy, the database you configure, the backups you run — is always your responsibility.

**Trap 3: Retroactive Liability**  
Non-compliance begins on Day 1, not on the day you are caught. Two years of illegal hosting meant two years of criminal liability, accumulated daily. The inspection did not create the problem — it revealed a problem that had existed since the contract was signed.

### Khmer

**សុវត្ថិភាព (Security) មិនមែនមានន័យថា អនុលោមភាព (Compliance) នោះទេ។ ហើយអនុលោមភាព ក៏មិនមែនមានន័យថា សុវត្ថិភាពនោះដែរ។ នៅប្រទេសបារាំង សម្រាប់ទិន្នន័យសុខភាព អ្នកចាំបាច់ត្រូវតែមានទាំងពីរ — ហើយពួកវាត្រូវឆ្លងកាត់ការផ្ទៀងផ្ទាត់ដាច់ដោយឡែកពីគ្នា។**

**អន្ទាក់ទី ១: ការយល់ច្រឡំលើវិញ្ញាបនប័ត្រ** — ISO 27001, SOC 2 និង HDS គឺជាវិញ្ញាបនប័ត្រខុសៗគ្នា និងមានគោលបំណងខុសគ្នា។  
**អន្ទាក់ទី ២: ការគិតថាអ្នកដទៃទទួលខុសត្រូវជំនួស** — វិញ្ញាបនប័ត្ររបស់ Cloud មិនមែនជាវិញ្ញាបនប័ត្ររបស់ Application ទេ។ Application Layer គឺជាការទទួលខុសត្រូវរបស់អ្នក។  
**អន្ទាក់ទី ៣: ការទទួលខុសត្រូវដែលគិតថយក្រោយ** — ការបំពានច្បាប់គឺរាប់ចាប់ពីថ្ងៃទី ១ មិនមែនរាប់ពីថ្ងៃដែលគេចាប់បាននោះទេ។

## ៣. ការបកប្រែទៅជាការអនុវត្ត (Translation to Practice)

```
Before signing with any cloud vendor for healthcare:

□ Ask: "What HDS Activities are you certified for?"
   Expected: Activities 1–3 (IaaS providers)
□ Confirm: You must certify for Activities 4–6 yourself
□ Verify Data Localisation: servers in France or EEA
□ Review contract clauses:
   □ Data location specified
   □ Audit rights for your organisation
   □ Incident notification to ANS
   □ Data portability / reversibility clause
□ Check subcontractors: Are they also HDS certified?
□ Timeline: Plan 10–15 months from zero to certification
```
