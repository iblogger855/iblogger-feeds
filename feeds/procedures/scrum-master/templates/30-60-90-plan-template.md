# Template: 30/60/90 Day Plan (New Scrum Master)

> **How to use:** Fill this out in your first week and review it with your manager. It turns the [First 90 Days procedure](../01-first-90-days.md) into a concrete, shareable commitment. As a Scrum Master you have *no authority* over the team — this plan is about how *you* show up and serve, not what you'll make the team do. Keep it to one page. Update it at each 30-day mark. The discovery questions below double as your 1-on-1 opener with every team member.

## ─── DISCOVERY QUESTIONS (new SM) ─────────────────────────────────

> Ask these of every team member and key stakeholder in your first two weeks. Same questions for each — patterns across answers reveal the real problems.

- What's working well about how we work that I should **not** change?
- What's the most frustrating part of your sprint?
- When you're blocked, what actually happens today?
- If you ran the retro, what's the first thing you'd want us to fix?
- Who tends to make the "how" decisions on this team?
- What does "a good sprint" feel like to you?

# 30/60/90 Plan — Vanna, Scrum Master

**Start date:** 2026-06-01
**Manager:** Sopheap (Engineering Manager)
**Team:** Team Lotus (6 engineers, 1 QA, shared PO)
**Mandate:** "Help Team Lotus actually *be* agile — right now it's Scrum-but: planning happens, but retros get skipped and nothing improves."

> Boundaries I will hold: I do not assign work, commit dates, or manage people.

**30 days:** 1-on-1 with all 8 members; observed every ceremony for two sprints. Headline from the maturity assessment (shared with the team first, at the sprint retro on day 26): retros skipped 4 of last 6 sprints; one senior dev (Sokha) makes nearly every "how" decision while three engineers stay silent; impediments die quietly in standup. Lowest scores: **Psychological safety (2)** and **Continuous improvement (2)**.

**60 days:** team chose its two focus areas — *make retros stick* and *spread the "how" decisions*. Stood up a visible impediment log. Team authored its first working agreements (retro every sprint, non-negotiable; round-robin in planning). Framed both as 2-sprint experiments.

**90 days:** facilitated Sprint 9 clean — first retro in months that produced an action the team actually felt (Sokha now writes the approach as an RFC for the team to react to, instead of deciding live). Re-ran the assessment: safety 2→3, kaizen 2→4, retro-action completion 0%→100% last two sprints.

**Need from Sopheap:** back me when I protect velocity from being read as a productivity score in the leadership sync.
