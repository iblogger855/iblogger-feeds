| 6 | Continuous improvement | 2 | (baseline) |

---

## 🔍 Per-Dimension Detail

### 1. Ceremonies — Score 3
- **Evidence:** Planning and standup happen reliably. But standup is a status report *to* the PO, and the review often slips to "we'll demo next time."
- **+1 looks like:** Standup is a team-to-team sync about flow; review demos working software every sprint.

### 3. Self-organization — Score 2
- **Evidence:** Sokha (senior dev) decides the technical approach for nearly every story, live in planning. Three engineers (Dara, Bopha, Veasna) rarely speak. Work is effectively assigned, not pulled.
- **+1 looks like:** The team breaks down and picks up work itself; the "how" decision is shared, not owned by one person.

### 5. Psychological safety — Score 2
- **Evidence:** In two retros' worth of observation, only 2–3 of 8 people spoke. After Sokha gives an opinion, the room goes quiet. Bad news (a missed integration) surfaced only at sprint end.
- **+1 looks like:** Quiet voices contribute; people disagree openly; problems surface early.

### 6. Continuous improvement — Score 2
- **Evidence:** Retros skipped in 4 of the last 6 sprints ("too busy"). The 2 that ran produced vague notes and zero completed actions. Same flaky-CI complaint in both.
- **+1 looks like:** Retro every sprint; 1–2 concrete actions; most get done and are reviewed.

---

## 🎯 Priorities (team chose, at the retro)
1. **Continuous improvement** — make retros stick; the team agreed nothing gets better while they're skipped.
2. **Self-organization / Safety** (linked) — spread the "how" decisions so more voices shape the work.

**Experiments to propose (time-boxed):**
- [ ] Retro every sprint, non-negotiable, varied format — try 3 sprints, inspect retro-action completion %.
- [ ] Sokha drafts the approach as a short RFC for the team to react to (instead of deciding live) — try 2 sprints, inspect how many engineers contribute in planning.
