# 🌀 Scrum Master Playbook

A practical, step-by-step playbook for someone stepping into a **Scrum Master role for the first time, in a new company/workspace.** It covers both halves of the job:

1. **Onboarding** — what *you* do in your first 90 days to land well.
2. **Process** — the facilitation, impediment-removal, coaching, and improvement practices you *bring* to the team.

> Built on one principle: **a Scrum Master leads by serving, not by directing.** You have no authority over the team — you don't assign work, commit dates, or manage people. You own the *process* and the team's *effectiveness*: facilitating ceremonies, removing impediments, coaching toward self-organization, and shielding the team so it can focus. Spend your first month earning trust and understanding the team's system before you change it.

Each procedure follows the house format: a **Mermaid swimlane diagram**, a portable **ASCII flow**, and a **step-by-step responsibility table** — so it works in GitHub, Confluence, or a terminal.

## 🧭 How to Use This Playbook

```
You just got the Scrum Master role
        │
        ▼
  Read 01 (First 90 Days) ── fill the 30/60/90 template
        │
        ▼
  Days 1–14:  LISTEN  → 1-on-1s, discovery questions, observe ceremonies
  Days 15–30: ASSESS  → 02 + Agile Maturity Assessment template
  Days 31–60: PLAN    → co-create improvement backlog + Impediment Log
  Days 61–90: EXECUTE → 03, 04, 05, 06 + retros that drive change
```

> **New to the role and short on time?** Read **01** today (especially *SM vs PM vs Team Lead*), skim **02**, and start your 1-on-1s tomorrow. Everything else can wait until you understand the team.
