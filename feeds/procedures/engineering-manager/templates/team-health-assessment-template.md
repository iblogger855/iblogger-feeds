# Team Health Assessment — Payments Squad · 2026-06-26

**Assessed by:** Mony, Engineering Manager
**Period observed:** 2026-06-01 → 06-25 (first 25 days)
**Method:** 1-on-1s with all 6 + delivery trend + last 3 retros + chats with PM Sothea and QA

## 📊 Scorecard

| # | Dimension | Score | Evidence |
|:-:|:----------|:-----:|:---------|
| 1 | People & morale | **2** | Lost a senior last quarter; team felt unheard after. Two people called the mood "drifting." No one is actively leaving today, but Pisey is restless. |
| 2 | Skills & gaps | **2** | Billing-service knowledge sits only with Dara (single point of failure). Junior Lina has no clear mentor. |
| 3 | Delivery | **3** | Ships roughly on time but firefights quality near releases. Predictability trend flat, not falling. |
| 4 | Team dynamics & safety | **2** | Problems are hidden until they break; postmortems lean toward blame. Two voices dominate planning. |
| 5 | Process | **3** | Ceremonies run but one status meeting has no clear purpose; "done" is inconsistently defined. |
| 6 | Stakeholder relationships | **3** | PM trusts the team but routes around it when anxious about dates. |

## 🎯 Top 3 Priorities
1. **Team dynamics & safety (2)** — caps everything else. Start blameless postmortems; reward early problem-surfacing; protect airtime in planning.
2. **People & morale (2)** — rebuild the sense of being heard via consistent 1-on-1s; backfill the lost senior.
3. **Skills & gaps (2)** — spread billing knowledge off Dara by pairing Pisey on the next billing epic.

## ✅ What's working (protect this)
- Strong release discipline from QA partnership; Dara's deep technical ownership.

## 🆘 What I need from my manager
- One backfill headcount for the departed senior; air cover on mid-sprint scope from Sales.

> Note: Rotha's individual performance gap is being handled privately via a growth plan and 1-on-1s — it is not detailed in this team-level report.
