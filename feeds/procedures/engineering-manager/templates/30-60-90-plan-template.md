# Template: 30/60/90 Day Plan (New Engineering Manager)

> **How to use:** Fill this out in your first week and review it with your manager. It turns the [First 90 Days procedure](../01-first-90-days.md) into a concrete, shareable commitment. Keep it to one page. Update it at each 30-day mark. Remember the core shift: your goals are about building the *team*, not shipping code yourself.

# 30/60/90 Plan — Mony, Engineering Manager

**Start date:** 2026-06-01
**Manager:** Vibol (Director of Engineering)
**Team:** Payments squad — 6 engineers (2 senior, 3 mid, 1 junior)
**Mandate:** "Stabilize the team — morale is shaky, we lost one senior last quarter, and Rotha isn't performing. Keep delivery steady while you fix the people side."

**30 days:** 1-on-1 with all 6; mapped partners (PM Sothea, Team Lead Dara, QA). Health assessment shared with Vibol by day 26 — headline: morale 2/5 (after the senior's exit nobody felt heard), psychological safety 2/5 (problems hidden until they break), delivery 3/5, skills 2/5 (billing knowledge sits only with Dara).

**60 days:** drafted growth plans with all 6 — junior Lina wants backend depth; mid Pisey is ready to stretch toward senior. Named the top-3: (1) rebuild safety, (2) spread billing knowledge off Dara, (3) address Rotha's performance fairly. Asked Vibol for one backfill headcount for the lost senior.

**90 days:** weekly 1-on-1s running with everyone; killed a pointless status meeting and gave that time back (the win the team felt most); started blameless postmortems. Began a real, supported growth plan with Rotha — direct early feedback, pairing with Dara, clear gap and a fair 60-day check-in, no surprises. Health signals live: delivery predictability trend, on-call fairness, engagement themes — all team-level, never per-person.

**Need:** the backfill req approved, and air cover to protect the team from mid-sprint Sales requests.
