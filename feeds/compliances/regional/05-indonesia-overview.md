# Indonesia — Compliance Overview for Digital Platforms

**Tags:** #compliance #indonesia #uupdp #ojk #bi #southeast-asia

---

## Summary

Indonesia is Southeast Asia's largest economy (270 million people) and has a rapidly growing digital sector. The Personal Data Protection Law (UU PDP, 2022) is now in force. The OJK (Financial Services Authority) and Bank Indonesia regulate the financial sector. Indonesia has significant data localisation requirements for certain sectors.

---

## Key Regulators

| Regulator | Acronym | Jurisdiction |
|:----------|:--------|:-------------|
| Ministry of Communication and Information Technology | Kominfo | Data protection (UU PDP), internet platforms |
| Financial Services Authority | OJK | Banking, insurance, securities, fintech |
| Bank Indonesia | BI | Payment systems, e-money |
| Financial Transaction Reports and Analysis Centre | PPATK | AML/CFT |
| Ministry of Health | Kemenkes | Healthcare regulation |

## Data Protection — UU PDP (2022)

Indonesia's Personal Data Protection Law (Undang-Undang Perlindungan Data Pribadi) came into force in October 2022, with a 2-year transition period (full compliance by October 2024).

| Requirement | Detail |
|:------------|:-------|
| Scope | Any party processing personal data of Indonesian residents |
| Personal data | General and specific (sensitive) categories |
| Specific/sensitive data | Financial data, health, biometrics, genetics, children's data, political, criminal records, religion |
| Legal basis | Consent, contract, legal obligation, vital interest, public task, legitimate interest |
| Data subject rights | Access, correction, deletion, portability, object, withdraw consent |
| DPO | Required for large-scale or sensitive data processing |
| Breach notification | Within 14 days to Kominfo + affected individuals |
| Cross-border transfer | Requires data protection level equivalent to UU PDP |
| Max penalty | Up to IDR 2% of annual revenue; criminal: up to 6 years imprisonment |

## Data Localisation

Indonesia has sector-specific localisation requirements:

| Sector | Requirement |
|:-------|:------------|
| Financial data (OJK regulated) | Data centres must have components in Indonesia |
| Government/strategic data | Must be stored in Indonesia |
| Electronic systems (large platforms) | Must have local data centre or mirroring |

Government Regulation No. 71/2019 requires **electronic system operators (ESOs)** — including large digital platforms — to have data centres and disaster recovery centres in Indonesia or use local providers.

## Payment Services — BI and OJK

| Service | Regulator | Licence |
|:--------|:----------|:--------|
| E-money | Bank Indonesia | BI licence |
| Payment gateway | Bank Indonesia | BI licence |
| Peer-to-peer lending | OJK | OJK registration |
| Fintech payment | Bank Indonesia | BI Fintech Regulatory Sandbox |

**QRIS** (Quick Response Code Indonesian Standard) — Indonesia's national QR payment standard. All licensed payment providers must support QRIS.

---

## AML/CFT — PPATK

| Obligation | Requirement |
|:-----------|:------------|
| Customer identification | KYC for all customers |
| EDD | For PEPs, high-risk customers |
| STR filing | PPATK — within 3 working days |
| Cash transaction reports | Transactions > IDR 500 million |
| Record retention | 5 years |

---

## Healthcare Regulations

| Requirement | Regulator |
|:------------|:----------|
| Hospital licensing | Ministry of Health |
| Doctor registration | Indonesian Medical Council (KKI) |
| Telemedicine | Permenkes 20/2019 (Telemedicine Regulation) |
| Medical device registration | BPOM (National Agency of Drug and Food Control) |
| Pharmaceutical | BPOM licensing |

Indonesia has a **formal telemedicine regulation** (Permenkes 20/2019) — one of the more advanced in Southeast Asia.
