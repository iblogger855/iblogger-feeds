# Africa Regional Compliance Overview

**Region:** Sub-Saharan Africa and North Africa  
**Tags:** #compliance #africa #popia #ndpr #southafrica #nigeria #kenya #data-protection

---

## Overview

Africa's data protection landscape is rapidly maturing. The African Union's Convention on Cyber Security and Personal Data Protection (Malabo Convention) provides a continental framework, but actual regulation happens at national level. South Africa and Nigeria have the most developed enforcement regimes.

## South Africa

### Key Authorities
- **Information Regulator** — enforces POPIA and PAIA
- **SARB** (South African Reserve Bank) — banking and financial regulation
- **FSCA** (Financial Sector Conduct Authority) — financial services conduct
- **ICASA** — telecoms and broadcasting regulator

### POPIA — Protection of Personal Information Act

**In force:** 1 July 2021 (1-year grace period ended 30 June 2022)  
**Applies to:** Any responsible party that processes personal information about data subjects in South Africa

#### Eight Conditions of Lawful Processing

```
1. Accountability — responsible party must comply with all conditions
2. Processing limitation — lawful grounds, adequate, relevant, not excessive
3. Purpose specification — collect for specific purpose, notify data subject
4. Further processing limitation — must be compatible with original purpose
5. Information quality — accurate, complete, up-to-date, not misleading
6. Openness — privacy notice to data subjects
7. Security safeguards — reasonable technical and organisational measures
8. Data subject participation — rights to access, correct, object, delete
```

#### Legal Grounds for Processing

| Ground | Description |
|:-------|:------------|
| Consent | Data subject consent |
| Contractual obligation | Processing necessary for contract |
| Legitimate interest | Responsible party or third party — subject to balancing |
| Legal obligation | Compliance with law |
| Vital interest | Protection of life |
| Public interest | Public law functions |

#### Special Personal Information (South Africa's Article 9)

```
Prohibited unless specific condition applies:
- Religious or philosophical beliefs
- Race or ethnic origin
- Trade union membership
- Political persuasion
- Health or sex life
- Biometric information
- Criminal behaviour
```

#### Key Requirements

```
□ Appoint Information Officer — register with Information Regulator
□ Privacy notice — what you collect, why, who you share with
□ Data subject rights — access (30 days), correction, objection, deletion
□ Cross-border transfers — only to countries with adequate protection
  OR specific contractual safeguards
□ Security safeguards — reasonable physical, technical, procedural
□ Security compromise notification — Regulator and data subjects
  as soon as reasonably possible after discovering breach
□ Direct marketing — opt-in for electronic communications
  (must provide opt-out in every message)
□ DPIA — recommended for high-risk processing
```

#### Penalties

| Violation | Maximum |
|:----------|:--------|
| Criminal offence (knowingly) | 10 years imprisonment or R10M fine |
| Civil enforcement | R10M (≈ $530K) |
| Regulator enforcement notice | Binding corrective action |

## Nigeria

### Key Authorities
- **NDPC** (Nigeria Data Protection Commission) — enforces NDPA (created 2023)
- **CBN** (Central Bank of Nigeria) — banking and fintech regulation
- **SEC** — capital markets
- **NCC** (Nigerian Communications Commission) — telecoms

### NDPA — Nigeria Data Protection Act 2023

**In force:** June 2023 (replaced NDPR 2019)  
**Applies to:** Data controllers and processors of personal data of Nigerian residents, regardless of location

#### Key Provisions

```
Lawful bases (aligned with GDPR):
□ Consent — freely given, specific, informed, unambiguous
□ Contractual necessity
□ Legal obligation
□ Vital interests
□ Public interest
□ Legitimate interests
```

#### Notable Requirements

```
□ Data Protection Officer — mandatory for high-risk processing
□ Privacy notice — concise, transparent, in plain language
□ Data subject rights — access, rectification, erasure, portability, objection
□ Data Protection Impact Assessment — for high-risk processing
□ Cross-border transfers — adequate protection OR safeguards
□ Data breach notification — NDPC within 72 hours
□ Data processors — must register with NDPC if processing >5,000 Nigerians/year
□ Compliance audit — NDPC can audit registered processors
```

#### Penalties

| Violation | Maximum |
|:----------|:--------|
| Non-compliance | 2% of annual gross revenue or ₦10M (whichever higher) |
| Sensitive data violations | 3% of annual gross revenue |
| Criminal offences | Fines + imprisonment up to 3 years |

### CBN Fintech Regulations

```
Key CBN requirements for fintech platforms:
□ Payment Service Bank licence or fintech framework authorisation
□ BVN (Bank Verification Number) — KYC anchor for Nigeria
□ NIN (National Identity Number) — required for financial services
□ Customer Due Diligence — tiered KYC matching account limits
   Tier 1: ₦50K/day limit — name, address, phone, BVN
   Tier 2: ₦200K/day — + valid ID document
   Tier 3: full KYC — ₦1M/day, physical address verification
□ Anti-money laundering: SCUML registration for non-bank entities
□ Suspicious Transaction Reports — NFIU within 24 hours
```

## Kenya

### Key Authorities
- **ODPC** (Office of the Data Protection Commissioner) — enforces DPA 2019
- **CBK** (Central Bank of Kenya) — banking and mobile money
- **CMA** (Capital Markets Authority) — capital markets

### DPA 2019 — Data Protection Act

**In force:** November 2019  
**Applies to:** Any person or entity that determines the purpose/means of processing personal data in Kenya

```
Key requirements:
□ Register with ODPC as data controller or processor
□ Appoint Data Protection Officer (mandatory for organisations above threshold)
□ Privacy notice — purpose, legal basis, data subject rights
□ Data subject rights — access, correction, deletion, portability
□ Data breach notification — ODPC and data subjects within 72 hours
□ Cross-border transfers — adequate protection or approved safeguards
□ Sensitive personal data — explicit consent or specific legal grounds
□ Children's data — verifiable parental consent for under-18
```

#### Penalties

| Violation | Maximum |
|:----------|:--------|
| Non-compliance | KES 3M (≈ $23K) or imprisonment up to 10 years |
| Sensitive data breach | KES 5M |

### CBK Mobile Money Regulations

```
Kenya's M-Pesa ecosystem — tight regulatory framework:
□ Payment Service Provider licence from CBK
□ E-money regulations: 100% reserve backing, float in approved institutions
□ KYC tiers matching transaction limits
□ Agent network regulations — agents subject to principal's KYC obligations
□ Interoperability: mandated API connectivity between payment providers
```

## Ghana

### Key Authority
- **Data Protection Commission** — enforces DPA 2012

### DPA 2012
Ghana's 2012 Data Protection Act predates GDPR but covers similar principles:

```
□ Register with Data Protection Commission
□ Processing limitation — lawful basis required
□ Data subject rights — access, objection, erasure
□ Security measures — reasonable technical and administrative safeguards
□ Cross-border transfers — adequate protection required
□ Sensitive data — explicit consent required
```

## Ethiopia

### Key Authority
- **INSA** (Information Network Security Agency) — cybersecurity and data protection enforcement

### Personal Data Protection Proclamation (2021)
Ethiopia enacted its first comprehensive data protection law in 2021:

```
Key requirements:
□ Lawful basis for processing personal data
□ Privacy notice and transparency
□ Data subject rights — access, correction, erasure
□ Data breach notification to INSA
□ Cross-border transfer restrictions
□ Sensitive personal data — stricter controls
```

## Pan-African Frameworks

### Malabo Convention — AU Convention on Cyber Security and Personal Data Protection
- Adopted 2014, came into force 2023 (after ratification threshold met)
- Requires member states to adopt data protection laws
- Sets minimum standards: lawful processing, data subject rights, oversight authority
- Only ~15 African Union member states have ratified

### ECOWAS Supplementary Act on Personal Data
- Regional framework for West Africa
- Soft law — influences member state legislation
- Covers: lawful basis, data subject rights, cross-border transfers

## What to Build for Africa Expansion

```
Minimum compliance for a platform serving Africa:

□ Identify which countries you process residents' data from
□ Register with each country's DPA (Nigeria NDPC, Kenya ODPC, SA Info Regulator)
□ Implement country-specific KYC tiers (especially CBN Nigeria tiers)
□ Privacy notice — cover all lawful bases across jurisdictions
□ Data subject rights portal — access, correction, erasure, portability
□ 72-hour breach notification mechanism with DPA contacts configured
□ Cross-border transfer safeguards — data localisation or adequacy assessment
□ For fintech: separate CBN, CBK authorisations — licensing path per country
□ SCUML registration (Nigeria) for non-bank financial services
□ BVN/NIN integration for Nigerian KYC
```
