# NIS2 — Network and Information Security Directive 2

**Jurisdiction:** European Union  
**In force:** 16 January 2023 (member states must transpose by 17 October 2024)  
**Replaces:** NIS Directive (2016)  
**Applies to:** Essential and important entities in critical sectors operating in the EU  
**Tags:** #compliance #nis2 #eu #cybersecurity #critical-infrastructure

---

## What It Is

NIS2 is the EU's core cybersecurity directive for critical infrastructure and digital services. It significantly expands the scope of the original NIS Directive (2016), adds stricter requirements, and increases penalties substantially. Unlike GDPR which focuses on data protection, NIS2 focuses on **operational resilience** and **incident reporting**.

---

## Who It Applies To

NIS2 divides entities into two categories:

### Essential Entities (stricter obligations)
- Energy (electricity, gas, oil, hydrogen)
- Transport (air, rail, water, road)
- Banking and financial market infrastructure
- Health (hospitals, labs, R&D, pharma manufacturing)
- Drinking water and wastewater
- Digital infrastructure (DNS, TLD, cloud, data centres, CDN, trust services)
- ICT service management (MSPs, MSSPs)
- Public administration
- Space

### Important Entities (slightly lighter obligations)
- Postal and courier services
- Waste management
- Chemicals manufacturing and distribution
- Food production and distribution
- Manufacturing (medical devices, electronics, machinery, vehicles)
- Digital providers (online marketplaces, search engines, social networking)
- Research organisations

**Size threshold:** Generally applies to medium and large enterprises (50+ employees or €10M+ turnover). Smaller entities may be included if their services are critical.

## Core Requirements

### 1. Risk Management Measures (Article 21)

All entities must implement:

```
□ Risk analysis and information system security policies
□ Incident handling (detection, response, recovery)
□ Business continuity (backup, disaster recovery, crisis management)
□ Supply chain security (security of relationships with direct suppliers)
□ Security in network and information systems acquisition, development, maintenance
□ Vulnerability disclosure and handling policies
□ Cybersecurity training and basic hygiene practices
□ Cryptography and encryption policies where appropriate
□ Human resources security, access control policies, asset management
□ Multi-factor authentication or continuous authentication solutions
□ Secure voice/video/text communications
□ Secured emergency communications
```

### 2. Incident Reporting (Article 23)

Stricter than NIS1:

| Stage | Timeframe | Content |
|:------|:----------|:--------|
| **Early warning** | Within **24 hours** of becoming aware | Is it suspected to be malicious? Is it cross-border? |
| **Incident notification** | Within **72 hours** | Initial assessment — severity, indicators of compromise |
| **Intermediate report** | On request from authority | Status update |
| **Final report** | Within **1 month** | Root cause, impact, measures taken |

### 3. Supply Chain Security

New in NIS2 — entities must assess security practices of their direct suppliers and service providers. This cascades through the supply chain.

### 4. Management Accountability

**Senior management is personally liable** for NIS2 compliance failures. They can be temporarily banned from management positions for serious violations.

---

## Supervisory Authorities

Each member state designates national competent authorities (NCAs). Cross-border cooperation through:
- **NIS Cooperation Group** — strategic coordination
- **CSIRT Network** — technical coordination
- **ENISA** — EU Agency for Cybersecurity — supports implementation

---

## Penalties

| Entity type | Maximum fine |
|:------------|:------------|
| Essential entities | €10M or 2% of global annual turnover (whichever higher) |
| Important entities | €7M or 1.4% of global annual turnover |
| Plus: personal liability for senior management |

---

## Relationship to Other Regulations

| Regulation | Relationship |
|:-----------|:------------|
| GDPR | NIS2 incidents affecting personal data also trigger GDPR breach notification |
| DORA | Financial sector entities covered by DORA are excluded from NIS2 financial sector provisions |
| CER Directive | Physical resilience — companion directive to NIS2's cyber resilience |

---

## What to Build

```
Minimum NIS2 compliance for a digital platform in scope:

□ Risk assessment — documented, annual
□ Security policy — approved by senior management
□ Incident response plan — with 24h/72h/1-month reporting capability
□ Business continuity plan — RTO/RPO defined, tested
□ Supply chain security assessments — direct suppliers
□ MFA on all critical systems
□ Vulnerability management — scan, patch, track
□ Security awareness training — all staff, annual
□ Encrypted backups — tested restoration
□ Penetration testing — annual
□ Register with national competent authority
□ Designate responsible management contact for NIS2
□ Board-level cybersecurity briefing — management accountability
```
