# Estimation and Planning Idioms

**Tags:** #english #idioms #planning #estimation #agile  
**Read Time:** ~5 min

> Planning meetings are full of idioms that sound casual but carry precise meaning. Misunderstanding them leads to missed deadlines, blown scope, and frustrated teams.

---

## "Ballpark" / "Ballpark figure"

A rough, approximate estimate — not a commitment. From baseball: somewhere within the stadium, not exact.

```
"I can't give you a precise number yet, but ballpark — 2 to 3 weeks."
"Just give me a ballpark. I'm not holding you to it."
```

**Important:** When someone asks for a ballpark, they want a rough range — not "I need more information." Give your best guess with a caveat.

---

## "In the weeds"

Stuck in too much detail — losing sight of the bigger picture. Or: deeply buried in complex implementation.

```
"We're getting in the weeds on edge cases. Let's step back and 
 agree on the happy path first."
"I've been in the weeds on this migration all week — finally coming up for air."
```

---

## "Scope creep"

When the requirements of a project gradually expand beyond the original agreement — usually without a formal change.

```
"This started as a one-day task but scope crept into a two-week feature."
"We need to push back on scope creep — every new requirement delays delivery."
```

---

## "Move the goalposts"

Changing the success criteria after work has already started — making it harder to finish.

```
"They keep moving the goalposts — we delivered what was agreed and 
 now they want three more things."
"Stop moving the goalposts. Let's lock the spec before we start building."
```

---

## "Cut scope" / "Descope"

Remove features or requirements from the current delivery to meet a deadline.

```
"To hit Friday's deadline, we'll need to cut scope. What can we defer?"
"Let's descope the CSV export — it's not blocking the launch."
```

---

## "Hero sprint"

A sprint where the team works extreme hours to hit a deadline — unsustainable and a sign of poor planning.

```
"We can't keep doing hero sprints every release. 
 We need to fix the planning process."
"Last quarter was a hero sprint and three people burned out."
```

---

## "Stretch goal"

An ambitious goal beyond the main commitment — reached only if everything goes perfectly.

```
"The stretch goal for this sprint is to also finish the notification system,
 but we're not committing to that."
```

---

## "Buffer"

Extra time added to an estimate to account for the unexpected.

```
"Add a day of buffer — there are always surprises with database migrations."
"We have no buffer in this timeline. Any slip and we miss the deadline."
```

---

## "On track" / "Off track"

Whether the work is proceeding as planned or falling behind.

```
"Are we on track for the Friday release?"
"We're off track — the integration is taking longer than estimated."
```

---

## "Slip" / "Slippage"

When a deadline is missed or pushed back.

```
"There's been a one-week slip on the payment feature."
"If this slips again, we'll need to have a difficult conversation with the client."
```

---

## "Capacity"

How much work the team can actually complete in a sprint — accounting for meetings, interruptions, and time off.

```
"Our capacity this sprint is reduced — two people are on leave."
"Don't overfill the sprint. Our real capacity is about 60 points."
```

---

## "Gold plating"

Adding unnecessary polish or features beyond what was asked for — spending time on things that don't add value.

```
"We're gold plating this. The client asked for a basic report, not a dashboard."
"Stop gold plating and ship it. We can improve it based on feedback."
```
