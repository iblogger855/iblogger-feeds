# Meeting Idioms Decoded

**Tags:** #english #idioms #meetings #communication  
**Read Time:** ~5 min

> Corporate English is full of phrases that sound meaningful but are often vague — or that mean something very specific if you know the code. Here's the decoder.

---

## "Let's take this offline"

**Meaning:** This topic is too detailed or too specific for the whole group. Let's discuss it separately after the meeting.

```
"That's a great point, but let's take that offline — 
 it's too deep for us to get into right now."
```

**What to do:** After the meeting, message that person and continue the discussion.

---

## "Circle back"

**Meaning:** Return to this topic later — either later in the same meeting or in a future conversation.

```
"Let's circle back to the deployment question after we've reviewed the spec."
"Can we circle back on this next week once we have more data?"
```

---

## "Level set"

**Meaning:** Make sure everyone has the same understanding of the situation before moving forward.

```
"Before we dive in, I want to level set on where we are 
 with the project and what we're deciding today."
```

---

## "Move the needle"

**Meaning:** Make meaningful progress on something.

```
"What can we do this sprint that will actually move the needle on performance?"
"We've been discussing this for weeks without moving the needle."
```

---

## "Bandwidth"

**Meaning:** Capacity to take on more work. Borrowed from networking — how much can you handle?

```
"Do you have bandwidth to pick this up this sprint?"
"I don't have the bandwidth for this right now — maybe next sprint."
```

---

## "Touch base"

**Meaning:** Have a quick check-in conversation.

```
"Can we touch base tomorrow about the API integration?"
"I'll touch base with the QA team to see if they're ready."
```

---

## "Parking lot"

**Meaning:** A place to put topics that are raised but out of scope for the current discussion — to be revisited later.

```
"That's an important point — let me put that in the parking lot 
 and we'll come back to it at the end."
```

---

## "Boil the ocean"

**Meaning:** Attempt to do something impossibly large. Usually used as a warning.

```
"We can't boil the ocean in one sprint — let's pick the three most impactful changes."
"The spec is trying to boil the ocean. We need to descope."
```

---

## "Low-hanging fruit"

**Meaning:** Easy wins that can be achieved quickly with little effort.

```
"Before we tackle the big refactor, let's go for the low-hanging fruit 
 — the slow queries we already know about."
```

---

## "Alignment"

**Meaning:** Everyone agreeing on the same goal or approach.

```
"I want to make sure we have alignment on the timeline before we commit."
"Are we aligned on the approach, or do we need more discussion?"
```

---

## "Synergy"

**Meaning:** When two things combine to produce a result greater than their individual parts. Often used vaguely in business to mean "working together."

Real usage: mostly a buzzword. When you hear it in a meeting, it usually means "collaboration" or "working together."

```
"There's real synergy between the two teams here." 
= "These two teams work well together."
```

---

## "Action item"

**Meaning:** A specific task that someone is assigned to do as a result of the meeting.

```
"Action item for Dara: investigate the rate limiting issue and report back by Thursday."
```

---

## "Hard stop"

**Meaning:** An absolute deadline — the meeting must end at this time because the person has another commitment.

```
"I have a hard stop at 3 PM — can we make sure we cover the key decisions before then?"
```

---

## "Punt"

**Meaning:** Postpone or push something to a later time. From American football — kicking the ball downfield to delay.

```
"Let's punt the billing discussion to next sprint — it's not blocking us right now."
"We punted on the mobile design until we have the web version stable."
```

---

## "Deep dive"

**Meaning:** A thorough, detailed exploration of a topic.

```
"Can we schedule a deep dive into the database performance issues?"
"I'll do a deep dive into the logs and report back."
```
