# Strategy 5: The Socratic Method

**Audience:** Capable learners who benefit from discovery over lecture.

**Goal:** Lead the learner to the correct conclusion through questions, not statements.

**The Formula:**
```
1. Ask a question whose answer leads toward the concept
2. Accept the answer — correct or not — and ask a follow-up
3. Each question narrows the space of possible answers
4. The learner "discovers" the solution themselves
5. Confirm: "Yes — and that's exactly what a [concept] does."
```

**Example — Teaching Binary Search:**
> *"You're looking for page 347 in a 700-page book. Do you start from page 1?"*  
> *"No, I'd open the middle."*  
> *"Why the middle?"*  
> *"Because I know if 347 is bigger or smaller than wherever I land."*  
> *"And once you know that?"*  
> *"I throw away half the book."*  
> *"Exactly. How many times can you cut 700 in half before you reach 1?"*  
> *"About 10."*  
> *"That's binary search. O(log n)."*

**When to use it:** Code review, 1-on-1 mentoring, teaching interviews, helping someone debug their own code without giving the answer.

**Signature move:** Never state — always ask. "What would happen if...?", "Why did you choose that?", "What's the simplest case?"
