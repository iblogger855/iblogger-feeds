# Strategy 2: Feynman Technique

**Audience:** Anyone — including yourself.

**Goal:** Expose gaps in your own understanding. If you can't explain it simply, you don't truly understand it.

**The Formula:**
```
1. Write the concept name at the top of a blank page
2. Explain it as if teaching a complete beginner (out loud or in writing)
3. Every time you get stuck or use jargon: STOP
4. Go back to the source material and fill the gap
5. Simplify. Replace jargon with plain language. Use analogies.
6. Repeat until you can explain it with zero jargon
```

**Example — Explaining Recursion:**
> *"A function that calls itself. But why would you do that? Imagine you're looking for your keys. You check your coat pocket. Not there. You check your bag. Inside the bag, there are pockets. You check those pockets. Inside one pocket, there's a smaller bag with its own pockets. You keep going deeper until you either find the keys or run out of places to check. Recursion is that process — a function that asks 'can I handle this directly? No? Then break it down and ask again.'"*

**When to use it:** Self-study, preparing to teach, reviewing a colleague's code, debugging your own mental model.

**Signature move:** "If I can't explain this without the word X, I don't understand it yet."
