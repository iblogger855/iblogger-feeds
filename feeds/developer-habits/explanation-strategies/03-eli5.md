# Strategy 3: ELI5 — Explain Like I'm 5

**Audience:** Complete newcomers, non-technical stakeholders, executives.

**Goal:** Make the concept tangible using objects and experiences everyone has.

**The Formula:**
```
1. Find ONE physical object or childhood experience that maps to the concept
2. Use ONLY that analogy. Don't mix metaphors.
3. Walk through the analogy completely before touching the real concept
4. Then say: "In software, X is exactly like that because..."
5. Use no jargon at all. If you must, immediately define it in one sentence.
```

**Example — Explaining a Message Queue:**
> *"Imagine a restaurant kitchen. Orders come in faster than the chefs can cook. Instead of yelling at the chefs to cook faster, you put the orders on a ticket rail — in order, first order first. The chefs take tickets from the left side, cook, and the rail slowly empties. The queue is that ticket rail. It absorbs the burst of incoming orders and smooths out the work."*

**Example — Explaining DNS:**
> *"You know how your phone has a contact named 'Mom' but the phone actually dials a number? DNS is exactly that. You type 'google.com' (the name) and DNS translates it to '142.250.80.46' (the number the internet actually uses)."*

**When to use it:** Product demos, client meetings, explaining incidents to management, onboarding non-engineers, README introductions.

**Signature move:** "Think of it like..." and then commit to the analogy completely.
