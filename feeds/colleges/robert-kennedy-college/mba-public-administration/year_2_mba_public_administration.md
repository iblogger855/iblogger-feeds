# Year 2: Core Business & Financial Management

Moving beyond theoretical foundations, Year 2 introduces the hard skills required to manage a public organization. This year blends traditional business administration concepts (like accounting and organizational behavior) with the unique ethical and financial constraints of the public sector.

---

## 1. Public Financial Management

*   **Taxation and Revenue Generation**
    *   *Focus:* Understanding how governments fund their operations. The principles of progressive vs. regressive taxation, tax enforcement, and the social contract of taxation.
*   **Public Budgeting**
    *   *Focus:* How public funds are allocated. Understanding deficit spending, municipal bonds, and the political battles that shape the national budget.

## 2. Organizational Behavior in the Public Sector

*   **Managing the Civil Service**
    *   *Focus:* Adapting corporate OB theories (Maslow, Tuckman) to government bureaucracies. How to motivate employees who may not be motivated by profit or bonuses, but by public service and job security.
*   **Navigating Bureaucratic Culture**
    *   *Focus:* Understanding institutional inertia. Why government agencies resist change and how modern leaders can foster innovation within strict legal constraints.

## 3. Public Sector Ethics and Law

*   **Conflicts of Interest**
    *   *Focus:* The strict ethical boundaries separating public duty from private gain. Navigating nepotism, lobbying, and the "revolving door" between government and industry.
*   **Administrative Law**
    *   *Focus:* The legal framework that governs the bureaucracy itself. Ensuring that government actions are constitutional, fair, and open to judicial review.

## 4. Data Literacy and Statistics

*   **Quantitative Methods for Public Policy**
    *   *Focus:* Using Excel, R, or Python to analyze demographic data, crime statistics, or public health metrics. Preparing students for the advanced data analytics required in Year 4.

---

## The Workload Expectation
Year 2 shifts toward practical, applied assignments. Students will analyze real-world municipal budgets, debate ethical case studies, and begin performing basic statistical regressions on public datasets.
