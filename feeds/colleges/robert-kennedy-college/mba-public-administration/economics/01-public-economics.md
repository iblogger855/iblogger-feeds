# Public Economics (UK Curriculum)

While private sector business focuses on maximizing shareholder wealth, Public Economics focuses on maximizing societal welfare. It examines the role of the government in the economy, particularly when free markets fail to produce efficient or equitable outcomes.

---

## 1. Market Failures and Government Intervention

*   **Public Goods**
    *   *Focus:* Goods that are non-excludable and non-rivalrous (like national defense, lighthouses, or clean air). Free markets will not provide these because you cannot easily charge people for them. The government must step in to fund them via taxation.
*   **Externalities (Positive and Negative)**
    *   *Focus:* When a transaction between a buyer and seller affects a third party. A factory polluting a river is a negative externality. Education is a positive externality. Governments use taxes (Pigouvian taxes) to discourage negative externalities and subsidies to encourage positive ones.

## 2. The Tragedy of the Commons

*   **Common Pool Resources**
    *   *Focus:* Resources that are non-excludable but rivalrous (like a public fishing lake or an open pasture). If left unregulated, rational individuals will overuse and destroy the resource, acting in their own short-term self-interest.
*   **Regulatory Solutions**
    *   *Focus:* How governments solve the tragedy of the commons through quotas, privatization, or strict usage regulations to ensure long-term sustainability.

## 3. Equity and Wealth Distribution

*   **The Welfare State**
    *   *Focus:* The economic arguments for and against the redistribution of wealth to ensure a baseline standard of living for all citizens.
*   **Efficiency vs. Equity Trade-off**
    *   *Focus:* The core dilemma of public economics: policies that increase total economic efficiency (like lowering corporate taxes) often reduce social equity, and vice versa.

---

## Key Frameworks & Tools

*   Pigouvian Taxes and Subsidies
*   Pareto Efficiency
*   The Lorenz Curve & Gini Coefficient (measuring inequality)
*   Game Theory (Prisoner's Dilemma applied to public goods)

---

## Why This Matters for Public Administration

Public administrators are the stewards of the economy. They must understand when to let the free market operate and when government regulation is absolutely necessary to protect shared resources and ensure the long-term survival of the state.
