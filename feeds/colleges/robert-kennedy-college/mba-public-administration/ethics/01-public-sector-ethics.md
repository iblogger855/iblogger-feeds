# Public Sector Ethics (UK Curriculum)

While business ethics often focus on corporate social responsibility and avoiding PR disasters, Public Sector Ethics is fundamentally about protecting the democratic system. A public administrator wields state power—the power to tax, the power to regulate, and the power to allocate massive resources. Ethics is the framework that prevents this power from being abused for private gain.

---

## 1. Core Principles of Public Ethics

*   **Impartiality and Objectivity**
    *   *Focus:* The civil servant must serve the public without bias, favoritism, or prejudice. Decisions (such as who gets a government contract or a building permit) must be based solely on objective criteria, not on personal relationships or political pressure.
*   **The Public Trust**
    *   *Focus:* The concept that holding a government office is not a right or a business opportunity, but a sacred trust. A violation of this trust (even a small one) erodes the entire foundation of democratic legitimacy.

## 2. Navigating Conflicts of Interest

*   **Identifying Conflicts**
    *   *Focus:* A conflict of interest occurs when a public official's private interests (financial, familial, or affiliations) could improperly influence the performance of their official duties. It is not just about actual corruption, but the *appearance* of corruption.
*   **Recusal and Divestment**
    *   *Focus:* The mechanisms used to resolve conflicts of interest. Teaching administrators when they must step away from a decision (recusal) or sell off private assets (divestment) to maintain impartiality.

## 3. Corruption and Whistleblowing

*   **Systemic Corruption**
    *   *Focus:* Understanding how corruption becomes institutionalized through bribery, extortion, and nepotism, and the devastating economic impact this has on developing nations.
*   **Whistleblower Protection**
    *   *Focus:* The ethical duty of civil servants to report illegal or unethical behavior within their own agencies, and the legal frameworks required to protect them from retaliation.

---

## Key Frameworks & Tools

*   The Nolan Principles of Public Life (Selflessness, Integrity, Objectivity, Accountability, Openness, Honesty, Leadership)
*   Conflict of Interest Matrices
*   Whistleblower Protection Acts

---

## Why This Matters for Public Administration

An unethical public administration destroys a country from the inside out. Foreign investors flee, taxes are evaded, and citizens lose faith in their institutions. MBAs in Public Administration must be trained to recognize ethical gray areas and possess the moral courage to make the right decision, even when it is politically or personally costly.
