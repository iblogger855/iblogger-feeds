# Year 1: Foundations of Public Administration & Governance

In a 4-year structured curriculum for Public Administration (adapted from Robert Kennedy College frameworks), Year 1 is designed to build the foundational knowledge required to understand how governments operate, how laws are made, and how economies function at a macro level.

---

## 1. Introduction to Public Policy

*   **Policy Formulation and Implementation**
    *   *Focus:* Understanding the lifecycle of public policy. How a societal problem is identified, debated, drafted into legislation, and eventually implemented by civil servants.
*   **The Unintended Consequences of Policy**
    *   *Focus:* Learning why well-meaning laws often fail or produce negative side-effects (e.g., rent control reducing housing supply). Introduces systems thinking.

## 2. Public Economics

*   **Microeconomics and Market Failures**
    *   *Focus:* Understanding why free markets sometimes fail to provide public goods (like clean air or national defense) and the economic justification for government intervention.
*   **The Tragedy of the Commons**
    *   *Focus:* Studying shared resources and the necessity of regulation to prevent the depletion of public goods by self-interested individuals.

## 3. Introduction to Political Science

*   **Systems of Governance**
    *   *Focus:* Comparative analysis of different political systems (democracies, autocracies, federal vs. unitary states) and how these structures impact administrative efficiency.
*   **The Separation of Powers**
    *   *Focus:* Understanding the balance between the Executive, Legislative, and Judicial branches, and where the bureaucracy fits into this power dynamic.

## 4. Academic Writing and Research Methods

*   **Evidence-Based Research**
    *   *Focus:* The cornerstone of university-level study. Teaching students how to read complex academic papers, analyze data sets, and write objective policy briefs.

---

## The Workload Expectation
Year 1 is heavily theoretical. Students are expected to read extensive historical texts and economic theories to build a strong mental model before they move into practical management and finance in Year 2.
