# Year 4: Advanced Policy, Ethics & Capstone

The final year of the curriculum prepares students to step into senior leadership roles. The focus shifts to modern governance tools, international standards, and completing a major capstone project that solves a real-world administrative problem.

---

## 1. Transparency and Evidence-Based Governance

*   **Open Data and Civic Tech**
    *   *Focus:* How making government ledgers and performance metrics public can reduce corruption and crowdsource auditing to the citizens.
*   **Outcome Auditing**
    *   *Focus:* Shifting the bureaucratic mindset from measuring outputs (money spent) to measuring outcomes (actual societal impact and value created).

## 2. Global Governance and Sustainability

*   **International Administration**
    *   *Focus:* Understanding how transnational organizations (like the UN, WHO, or World Bank) operate, and how global treaties affect local public administration.
*   **Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)**
    *   *Focus:* Integrating the UN's SDGs into municipal and national planning, ensuring that economic growth does not compromise environmental or social equity.

## 3. Advanced Public Policy Analysis

*   **Cost-Benefit Analysis (CBA)**
    *   *Focus:* The rigorous mathematical evaluation of proposed policies to determine if the social benefits outweigh the financial and social costs.
*   **Program Evaluation**
    *   *Focus:* Assessing existing government programs to determine if they should be expanded, reformed, or defunded based on empirical evidence.

## 4. The Senior Capstone / Dissertation

*   **Independent Research Project**
    *   *Focus:* The culmination of the 4-year degree. Students must partner with a real government agency, NGO, or public institution, identify a significant operational or policy challenge, and deliver a comprehensive, actionable thesis or consulting report to solve it.

---

## The Workload Expectation
Year 4 is highly independent. While classroom time decreases, students spend hundreds of hours researching, conducting field interviews, analyzing data, and writing their final Capstone dissertation, proving they are ready to lead in the public sector.
