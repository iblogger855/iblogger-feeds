# Public Financial Management (UK Curriculum)

Public Financial Management (PFM) is the bedrock of state power. It encompasses how a government raises revenue, allocates funds, and manages its debt. Unlike corporate finance, which seeks to maximize return on investment, public finance seeks to maximize social value while maintaining macroeconomic stability.

---

## 1. Revenue Generation and Taxation

*   **Principles of Taxation**
    *   *Focus:* The philosophy of why we pay taxes. Exploring concepts of fairness: progressive taxation (taxing the rich at a higher rate) vs. regressive taxation (like sales tax, which disproportionately affects the poor).
*   **Tax Enforcement and Compliance**
    *   *Focus:* The administrative challenge of collecting taxes, preventing evasion, and building a social contract where citizens feel their taxes are used effectively.

## 2. Public Budgeting

*   **The Budgeting Process**
    *   *Focus:* How governments decide what to spend money on. The shift from traditional line-item budgeting (which just tracks inputs) to performance-based budgeting (which tracks outcomes and social impact).
*   **Deficit Spending and Sovereign Debt**
    *   *Focus:* When and why governments borrow money (e.g., municipal bonds, sovereign debt). Understanding Keynesian economics: spending during recessions to stimulate the economy versus austerity measures.

## 3. Financial Auditing and Accountability

*   **Public Sector Audits**
    *   *Focus:* Ensuring that taxpayer money is not wasted or stolen. The role of Supreme Audit Institutions (like the National Audit Office in the UK) in verifying both financial accuracy and Value for Money (VfM).

---

## Key Frameworks & Tools

*   Progressive vs. Regressive Tax Models
*   Performance-Based Budgeting (PBB)
*   Keynesian Multiplier Effect
*   Municipal Bond Yield Analysis

---

## Why This Matters for Public Administration

Without money, government is just an idea. Administrators must be highly skilled in PFM to ensure that ambitious social policies are actually affordable. Furthermore, transparent and fair financial management is the primary way a government maintains the trust of its taxpayers.
