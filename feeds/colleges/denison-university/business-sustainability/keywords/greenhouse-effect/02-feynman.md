# Greenhouse Effect — Feynman Explanation
# បែបផែនផ្ទះក្រញ — ការពន្យល់បែប Feynman

*Author: ichamrong | Date: 2026-05-29*

---

Have you ever been inside a car parked in the sun? The air inside gets much hotter than the air outside, even on a day that is not that warm. That is because the glass lets sunlight in but slows the heat from escaping. The air inside the car gets trapped and heats up.

Planet Earth works the same way. That is the **greenhouse effect** (បែបផែនផ្ទះក្រញ).

Here is how it works, step by step:

**Step 1:** The Sun sends light to Earth. This light is bright, short, and energetic — like thin, fast waves. It passes right through the air without being stopped.

**Step 2:** The light hits the ground, the rice paddy, the lake, the roof of your house. The surface soaks up the energy and heats up.

**Step 3:** The warm ground now sends energy back upward — but this time as heat, not as visible light. Heat moves in long, slow waves called infrared radiation (វិទ្យុអ៊ីនហ្រ្វារ៉ូស). You cannot see it, but you can feel it if you hold your hand above a hot road.

**Step 4:** Certain gas molecules in the air — CO₂ (carbon dioxide — ឧស្ម័នកាបូនឌីអ៊ូស៊ីត), water vapor (ចំហាយទឹក), and methane (CH₄ — ម៉ីតាន) — absorb these slow heat waves. The molecules jiggle and vibrate. Then they re-send the heat in all directions — some up into space, but some back down toward the ground.

**Step 5:** The ground receives that extra returned heat and gets warmer. Warmer than it would be with no atmosphere at all.

Without any greenhouse gases, Earth would be frozen at about −18°C. With the natural amount of greenhouse gases, Earth sits at a comfortable +15°C. The greenhouse effect is not a bad thing — it is what makes Earth livable!

**The problem** is that humans are adding *extra* greenhouse gases. Every time a factory in Phnom Penh burns diesel, it adds CO₂. Every flooded rice paddy in Kampong Thom adds methane. Every truck driving garments to the port adds CO₂. These extra gases thicken the "glass" around Earth, trapping more heat, warming the planet.

As Earth warms:
- The rainy season becomes less predictable — Cambodian farmers can't tell when to plant
- The Mekong floods more violently some years, less predictably in others
- The sea rises — coastal areas in Koh Kong are at risk
- Extreme heat during the hot season becomes more dangerous for outdoor workers and rice crops

The greenhouse effect itself is natural and essential. The *enhanced* greenhouse effect — from our extra emissions — is the problem we need to solve.
