## The Simple Explanation / ការពន្យល់សាមញ្ញ

Imagine you and your friend share a wall in an apartment building. Your friend decides to practice drumming every night at 11 pm. He enjoys it — that is good for him. You pay him nothing and he charges you nothing. But you cannot sleep. Your headache is real. Your lost sleep is real. Yet none of that cost ever shows up in your friend's decision to drum.

That is a negative externality. An activity that is fine — or even profitable — for the person doing it, but silently costs other people who never agreed to pay.

Now scale it up. A factory makes cloth. The owner calculates: cotton costs this much, workers cost this much, electricity costs this much. He sets his price to cover all of that and make a profit. But the dye he uses goes into the river. The fisherman three kilometres away suddenly has no fish. The farmer whose rice paddy borders the canal watches his crop wilt. These people bear costs. But those costs were never in the factory owner's calculation. So the factory makes more cloth than it would if the full cost — including the ruined fish and the dead rice — were counted. That extra cloth is produced at a price that is dishonestly cheap.

នោះហើយជាផលប៉ះពាល់ខាងក្រៅអវិជ្ជមាន។ សកម្មភាពដែលល្អ — ឬសូម្បីតែចំណេញ — សម្រាប់អ្នកដែលធ្វើ ប៉ុន្តែសម្ងាត់ចំណាយលើអ្នកដទៃដែលមិនដែលព្រមទូទាត់។

## The Analogy / ការប្រៀបប្រដូច

Think of a food stall on a busy Phnom Penh street that burns coal to keep a pot boiling. The smoke drifts to the neighbouring stall. The vendor next door coughs all day. The coal-burning vendor's bill only shows the cost of coal, food, and rent — not the health of the neighbour. The neighbour pays with her lungs. That hidden payment is the negative externality.

គិតអំពីស្ដានហ្នឹងអាហារនៅតាមផ្លូវមមាញឹកនៅភ្នំពេញដែលដុតធ្យូងដើម្បីបន្ដើរឆ្នាំង។ ផ្សែងហូរទៅស្ដានហ្នឹងជិតខាង។ អ្នកលក់ជិតខាងក្អួតពេញមួយថ្ងៃ។ វិក្កយបត្ររបស់អ្នកលក់ដុតធ្យូងបង្ហាញតែការចំណាយធ្យូង អាហារ និងថ្លៃជួលប៉ុណ្ណោះ — មិនបង្ហាញសុខភាពរបស់ជិតខាងទេ។ ជិតខាងបង់ជាមួយសួត​របស់នាង។ ការបង់ប្រាក់លាក់ក្នុងនោះហើយជាផលប៉ះពាល់ខាងក្រៅអវិជ្ជមាន។

## Now Add the Complexity / ភាពស្មុគស្មាញ

The real challenge is not identifying that negative externalities exist — it is measuring them and assigning responsibility. When a garment factory discharges dye into the Mekong River basin, who bears the cost? It is diffuse: fishing communities, rice farmers, downstream communities in Vietnam, future generations. Economists call this the social cost, and the gap between the private cost the factory pays and the social cost everyone bears is called the externality. Correcting it requires either pricing the harm (a pollution tax), regulating the output directly, or creating property rights clear enough that the harmed parties can bargain with the producer. Each approach has trade-offs, and Cambodia's institutional capacity to implement any of them is still developing.

បញ្ហាពិតប្រាកដគឺមិនមែននៅការកំណត់ថាផលប៉ះពាល់ខាងក្រៅអវិជ្ជមានមានស្រាប់ — វាស្ថិតនៅការវាស់វែងនិងការកំណត់ទំនួលខុសត្រូវ។ នៅពេលដែលរោងចក្រកាត់ដេររំដោះថ្នាំប染ចូលទ្រង់ទ្រាយទន្លេមេគង្គ តើអ្នកណាទទួលបន្ទុកការចំណាយ? វារាលដាល: សហគមន៍នេសាទ កសិករស្រូវ សហគមន៍ខាងក្រោមទន្លេនៅវៀតណាម ជំនាន់ក្រោយ។ អ្នកសេដ្ឋកិច្ចហៅនេះថាការចំណាយសង្គម ហើយចន្លោះរវាងការចំណាយឯកជនដែលរោងចក្របង់ និង​ការចំណាយសង្គមដែលគ្រប់គ្នាទទួលបន្ទុកត្រូវបានគេហៅថាផលប៉ះពាល់ខាងក្រៅ។
