# Ecosystem Services — Feynman Explanation
# សេវាកម្មបរិស្ថាន — ការពន្យល់បែប Feynman

*Author: ichamrong | Date: 2026-05-29*

---

Imagine the Tonle Sap lake (បឹងទន្លេសាប) is your family's giant kitchen garden, but nobody planted it — nature did.

Every rainy season, the lake floods the forest. The fish swim into the flooded trees, eat fruits and insects, grow big, then swim back out when the water drops. Your family nets them for breakfast, lunch, and dinner. That fish on your rice plate? The forest made it. The water cycle made it. You didn't pay anyone to make it happen.

**That is an ecosystem service** (សេវាកម្មបរិស្ថាន) — a free service that nature does for people.

Think about what a lake actually does for a family living nearby:

- It gives you **fish to eat** (provisioning — ការផ្គត់ផ្គង់)
- It soaks up flood water so your house doesn't go underwater every year (regulating — ការគ្រប់គ្រង)
- It is beautiful — a good place to pray and relax (cultural — វប្បធម៌)
- Tiny creatures under the water make soil rich so you can grow vegetables near the shore (supporting — គ្រឹះ)

Now, here is the tricky part: none of these services show up in the price of fish at Phsar Thmei market. You pay for the fish, but you do not pay for the lake, the trees, the flood cycle, or the soil. They are "free." But they are only free if you keep the lake healthy.

What happens when someone drains the lake to make a shrimp farm? The fish are gone. The floods get worse because there is nothing to soak the water. The families who fished for generations have nothing. The shrimp farm makes money for a few years, then the soil dies and it is abandoned.

The lake was doing hundreds of millions of dollars of work every year — silently, invisibly, for free. And nobody noticed until it was gone.

**The lesson:** "Free" does not mean "worthless." Nature works for us every single day. We just forgot to write it in our notebooks.

Cambodia's forests in the Cardamom Mountains (ភ្នំកាដាម៉ូម) clean water for rice farmers downstream. The mangroves in Koh Kong protect fishing villages from ocean storms. The Mekong's seasonal floods bring nutrients that fertilize rice paddies without any fertilizer bag needed.

When we cut the trees or dam the river without thinking, we are firing the workers we never paid — and only then do we realize how much work they were doing.
