# FPIC / Free, Prior and Informed Consent — Feynman Explanation
# ការទទួលបានការយល់ព្រមជាមុនដោយសេរី — ការពន្យល់បែប Feynman

*Author: ichamrong | Date: 2026-06-01*

---

Imagine someone wants to build a road straight through your family's rice fields and the orchard your grandparents planted. Now imagine they don't ask you. They get a paper from an office in the city saying the land is theirs to use, and one morning the machines arrive.

You'd feel something deeply wrong happened — not just that you lost land, but that nobody let you *decide*. That feeling is exactly what **FPIC** is built to protect. FPIC stands for **Free, Prior and Informed Consent**, and it's a simple, powerful idea: *before you do something on people's land that will change their lives, you need their real yes — and they're allowed to say no.*

---

The clever part is in those four words. Each one closes a loophole that powerful people use to pretend they got permission when they didn't.

**Free** means nobody twisted your arm. If soldiers stand around while you "agree," or someone slips money to the village chief to say yes, that's not free. Real consent has no fear and no bribe behind it.

**Prior** means they ask *before*, not after. Asking once the bulldozers are already idling at the edge of the field isn't really asking — the answer is already decided. Prior means early, with enough time for the whole community to talk it over the way they normally make decisions.

**Informed** means you actually understand what you're agreeing to. If they tell you "we'll build something small and helpful" but hide that it will dry up your river, your yes was based on a lie. Informed means the full truth, in your own language, including the bad parts.

**Consent** is the big one. It means you can say **no**. This is what separates FPIC from a fake version where they "consult" you — listen politely, then do whatever they planned anyway. With FPIC, no means no.

---

Here's the core idea in one sentence: **FPIC turns a community from something that things are done *to*, into someone whose permission you actually need.**

That's a huge shift. Without FPIC, a company asks the government and ignores the people. With FPIC, the people are at the table, holding a real "no" in their hands.

---

Why should a *business* care, not just out of kindness? Because ignoring it backfires. Communities that were bulldozed without consent protest, sue, block roads, and tell the world. Banks now refuse to fund projects that skipped FPIC. A project can have every legal permit and still collapse because it never earned the *people's* permission — what businesses call the "social license to operate." The legal paper opens the gate; the community's real yes keeps it open.

---

**The simple version:**

FPIC means: before you do something big on people's land, get their genuine yes — given freely, asked early, based on the full truth, and with a real right to refuse. It treats the people who'll bear the consequences as decision-makers, not obstacles. And for a company, it's not just the right thing — it's the only way to build something that actually lasts.
