# Cap and Trade — Feynman Explanation
# ប្រព័ន្ធកំណត់កម្រិត និងការជួញដូរ — ការពន្យល់បែប Feynman

*Author: ichamrong | Date: 2026-06-01*

---

Imagine a village pond where everyone fishes. If anyone can take as many fish as they like, the pond is soon empty. So the village does something clever. It decides the pond can safely give up, say, 1,000 fish a year and no more. Then it prints exactly 1,000 fishing tokens. To catch one fish, you must hand in one token. No token, no fish.

That number — 1,000 — is the **cap**. It is a hard ceiling on how much can be taken. Now here is the second half: the villagers are allowed to **buy and sell their tokens**. That is the **trade**.

A **cap-and-trade** system for carbon works the same way. The "fish" is a tonne of carbon a factory is allowed to emit. The government sets the cap and hands out that many permits. To emit, you need a permit. And you can buy or sell them.

---

Here is the whole idea in one sentence: **the government decides exactly how much pollution is allowed in total, then lets companies trade the right to do it — so the pollution gets cut wherever it is cheapest.**

Why does the trading matter so much? Because companies are not all alike.

---

Picture two factories, each given 100 permits. Factory A can cut its pollution very cheaply — maybe it just switches a machine. Factory B cannot; cutting would cost it a fortune.

So Factory A cuts a lot, uses only 60 permits, and has 40 left over. Factory B does not want to spend a fortune cutting, so it buys those 40 permits from Factory A. Factory A makes money selling permits it didn't need. Factory B avoids an expensive cut by paying for permits instead.

And the total pollution? Still exactly 200 permits' worth — the cap held firm. But the *cutting* all happened at Factory A, where it was cheap. Nobody ordered this. The two factories sorted it out themselves, because trading let them.

---

Now, where does the price of a permit come from? Nobody sets it. It comes from the buying and selling, just like the price of fish at the market. If pollution is hard to cut everywhere, permits are precious and the price is high. If cutting turns out to be easy, permits are cheap. The price is the market telling everyone how hard the cap is to meet.

---

**The simple version:**

Decide the total pollution allowed — that's the cap. Print that many permits. Let companies trade them. The cap guarantees you hit your environmental target exactly, and the trading guarantees the cutting happens where it's cheapest. A carbon tax sets the price and lets the amount move; cap-and-trade sets the amount and lets the price move. Same goal, opposite handle.
