# Positive Externality — Analogy Bridge
# ផលជះក្រៅប្រព័ន្ធវិជ្ជមាន — ស្ពានប្រៀបធៀប

*Author: ichamrong | Date: 2026-06-01*

---

## Analogy 1: The Neighbor's Flower Garden / សួនផ្ការបស់អ្នកជិតខាង

**The Story:**
A woman plants a glorious flower garden in front of her house, purely for her own pleasure. But every passerby enjoys the colour, the bees pollinate the whole lane's fruit trees, and the street becomes a place people want to walk. She paid for all of it; the neighbourhood enjoys most of it for free. If she counted only her own pleasure, she'd plant a small bed. If the street chipped in for all the joy she creates, she'd plant a magnificent one. That gap — between the small garden she plants alone and the grand one the street would want — is the underprovision a positive externality causes.

**The Mapping:**

| Analogy Element | Positive Externality Element | ខ្មែរ |
|---|---|---|
| Her own pleasure in the garden | Private benefit | ផលប្រយោជន៍ឯកជន |
| Joy and bees for the whole lane | External benefit | ផលប្រយោជន៍ខាងក្រៅ |
| The small bed she plants alone | Underprovided market quantity | បរិមាណផ្គត់ផ្គង់តិច |
| Neighbours chipping in | Subsidy → optimal quantity | ឧបត្ថម្ភ → បរិមាណល្អបំផុត |

**Where the Analogy Breaks Down:**
A garden's beauty fades at the lane's end. Some externalities — a vaccine, stored carbon — spread invisibly across whole populations or the entire planet.

## Analogy 2: The Lighthouse Keeper / អ្នកថែប្រាសាទភ្លើង

**The Story:**
A keeper lights a lamp on a headland to guide his own family's fishing boat home. But every boat at sea that night is steered safely by the same beam — none of them paid for the oil. The keeper, counting only his family's boat, might light a small lamp. The whole fleet would want a blazing beacon. He can't charge the passing boats, so he under-lights. The beam's benefit leaks to everyone who can see it.

**The Mapping:**

| Analogy Element | Positive Externality Element | ខ្មែរ |
|---|---|---|
| Guiding his own boat | Private benefit | ផលប្រយោជន៍ឯកជន |
| Guiding the whole fleet free | External benefit to third parties | ប្រយោជន៍ដល់ភាគីទីបី |
| Can't bill passing boats | Cannot capture the spillover | មិនអាចទារផលជះ |
| The small lamp he lights | Underprovision | ការផ្គត់ផ្គង់តិចពេក |

**Where the Analogy Breaks Down:**
A lighthouse's beam is impossible to withhold from anyone — that makes it nearly a pure public good, an extreme case of positive externality rather than a typical one.

## Analogy 3: Teaching One Person to Read / ការបង្រៀនមនុស្សម្នាក់ឲ្យចេះអាន

**The Story:**
A teacher teaches a village child to read. The child gains directly. But that literate child grows up to fill in forms for illiterate elders, read medicine labels for neighbours, run an honest shop, vote thoughtfully. The whole village is lifted a little by one literate person. The family weighs only their child's gain against the cost of schooling — so without help, fewer children are taught than the village's full benefit would justify.

**The Mapping:**

| Analogy Element | Positive Externality Element | ខ្មែរ |
|---|---|---|
| The child's own gain | Private benefit | ផលប្រយោជន៍ឯកជន |
| The village lifted by literacy | External benefit | ផលប្រយោជន៍ខាងក្រៅ |
| Family weighs only its cost | Decision on private margin | ការសម្រេចលើរឹមឯកជន |
| Free or subsidized schooling | Pigouvian subsidy | ឧបត្ថម្ភធន Pigou |

**Where the Analogy Breaks Down:**
Education's external benefits unfold over decades and are hard to measure, making the "right" subsidy far harder to calculate than the analogy suggests.

---

## Core Insight Across All Three Analogies / ការយល់ដឹងស្នូល

All three reveal the same pattern: **when the good of an action spills onto bystanders who don't pay, the doer counts only their own slice and does too little of a wonderful thing.** The garden is too small, the lamp too dim, the schooling too scarce. The fix is always the same logic — reward the activity (a subsidy) until society gets the generous amount it truly wants. This is the bedrock argument for public funding of vaccines, education, reforestation, and clean energy.
